## Online Dangers | 213
The constant connection of **PCs** to the internet **has** opened **enormous opportunities** for criminal activity.
"Malware" is a term used to describe **malicious** software that finds its way onto **people's computers**.
It can be **spread** via:
- E-mail,
- online **services**,
- peer-to-peer file sharing,
- instant messaging **programs**.
The largest target of malware is the smartphone.
The most common **types** of malware are:

1. **VIRUS**
   Is a **malicious** program that **infiltrates** the PC and **causes** it to **malfunction** as **if it** was "ill".
2. **SPYWARE**
   Is a secret program that **observes and** reports on a user's online **behaviour**, without the person knowing it;
   it may also change a computer's settings or display pop-up ads.
3. **SPAM**
   Is **unwanted** e-mail **messages transmitted** to masses of people **usually** for **advertising** purposes.
   Sometime **harmless** but often they can carry a virus.
4. **WORM**
   Is a program that **invades** computers on a network and **buries** itself deep inside the software.
   It **replicates** itself to prevent deletion and may carry a virus or interfere with the computer's **operation**.
5. **TROJAN**
   Is a program that enters a PC by hiding in an **apparently** safe application; once inside the computer, the application can take control over the PC.
6. **BOTNET**
   Is a large group of **PCs** under control of a criminal gang, without the owner's **knowledge**.
7. **PHISHING**
   Is a method of tricking **people** into **revealing** sensitive information; they **usually** take **the** form of a message **which** looks like a genuine message.

## Social and ethical problems of IT
The following are some of the social and ethical **issues**:

### The digital divide
The society caused a split **between people** who are **technologically** literate, and have the **tools** to prosper in the modern world.
This is **an** economic division, **because technology is** expensive, but this is also affected by the age and education of the person;
**older** and poorer **people feel** left behind.

### Interpersonal relations
New **methods** of **communication** have changed how people interact with:
- business **meetings**,
- **friendship**,
- romance.
Social networks have become an essential method of keeping in touch for **millions** of people;
however, this **expansion creates** less human contact with others.
This impersonality and anonymity create a loss of empathy with **others**, creating a **phenomenon** of internet trolling.

### Information overload
Data can easily be shared online, meaning that we are creating **a** constantly growing mass of information now **available** on the internet.
With all of this information, we can't **know** for sure if **the** information is **reliable** or misleading.
**Because** we are constantly updated, we are **flooded with** e-mails, **causing** stress and **obstructing** productive work.
**Because** mobile **technology** is **convenient**, workers **find** themselves in a position where they are on call 24h a day.

### Censorship
Some **people** want to keep the internet as a place where anybody is free to publish **anything**.
In **their view**, society makes **progress through** the free exchange of ideas.
**Others** fear that **subversive** or immoral **opinions** or materials may have **damaging** effects on society, **especially** on **vulnerable** people such as children.

### Surveillance
The smartphone we carry is the most sophisticated tracking device we have ever invented.
Governments, **businesses**, and criminal **organizations** now have access to **enormous** information **about** us.

### Unemployment
The development of new **technology leads to the disappearance** of many jobs.
**Robots** and automation have eroded manual production work in factories.
Shop **assistants** and bank clerks are being replaced by **automated** systems.
Professional jobs are now in **threat** due to advances in AI software.
New **technology creates** new **professions** and **takes** over **others, rendering them** obsolete.

### Erosion of cultural industries
The internet is perfect for spreading infinite copies at no cost **of** products.
This is creating **devastating effects** on traditional music and film **industries**, creating a **steep** decline in sales of CDs and DVDs.
Pirated copies of songs and films **do** not feel like stealing.

## IT and the LAW

### COPYRIGHT PROTECTION
Copyright is a collection of rights granted to:
- **artists**,
- authors,
- software writers,
- musicians,
- other creators.
This law **prohibits** the **unauthorized** copying **and** distribution of:
- **books**,
- **computer** programs,
- **designs**,
- musical compositions.

### PATENT PROTECTION
With **a** patent, an inventor can take an invention and, by **officially** registering it in the national patent office, the invention **gains** an exclusive legal right for the commercial exploitation of the invention for a fixed time where nobody can create **copies** of it **without** permission.

### DATA PROTECTION
**The collection** of data **has** raised a concern about privacy.
Personal information can be shared worldwide.
Data protection **laws** deal with the **electronic** storage of personal information.
In many **countries**, there are legal **obligations** for organizations that hold and process personal data, including the right of people to see the data relating to them.

### DEFAMATION
**Defamation** is the crime of making a false **statement** about someone **which** damages **a** person's reputation.
1. It can be done in a written form called "libel".
2. It can be done in a spoken form called "slander".
Online **defamation** is present in online social media like Twitter and Facebook, in addition **to** chatrooms, blogs, and websites.

### ILLEGAL CONTENT
The law **prohibits** storing or **distributing certain** types of material online, **typically** related to terrorist activities or **the encouragement** of crimes and **violence**, **Child Pornography**, and Sexual Abuse.

### Cybercrime
There are laws against **hacking** and **the** theft of classified information.
**Those** are:
- fraud,
- **extortion**,
- identity **theft**,
- **spreading** viruses and other malware,
- harassment like **cyberbullying** and cyberstalking.

## The Web Today
The first websites **were** places where people went to find **information**:
- content of books,
- **magazines**,
- timetables...
Since **the** 1990s, a new generation of sites **was** born **called Web** 2.0;
instead of being passive **consumers**, people **became** active participants in the development of the site.
The web today is used for:

### Information sharing
The Wikipedia encyclopedia is a vast **collection** of factual information updated by the people.
Similar **sites were** born with the same idea, called **wikis** ("quick" in **Hawaiian**).

### Social Network
Popular sites have **given** people the possibility to set **up** their own personal page and use it to contact very old friends or make new **ones** by exchanging messages, music, photos, and **videos**.
**The most** famous **ones** are Facebook, MySpace, and Google+.

### Blogging
A blog is **an** abbreviation of "weblog"; the blog is a personal website **which** can be **used** as a daily diary, with information on current events and **commentary**.
Most **blogs** include:
- links to **other sites**,
- **photos**,
- graphics,
- audio **and video**.
Blogs are **usually** interactive, with the user **allowed** to leave a comment.
Twitter is simpler, **an** informal version of a blog with shorter messages.
A forum is **an** online discussion site in **which a** group of people who share the same interest can exchange **opinions and information**.

### Shopping
Many online shops sell things on the net like:
- music,
- films,
- books.
There are auction sites where you can sell your own goods, **like eBay**.
Large **chains** or smaller **shops** have their own website selling **an enormous and various** range of goods.
Many shoppers **regularly** check on the internet before buying **expensive articles**.

### Virtual Worlds and Gaming
Virtual **worlds** like Second Life, **which** is a recreation of our world in **which through** the computer it is possible to live and interact with other **users** by controlling a character called **an** avatar.
Social Gaming sites like Zynga offer a range of more traditional **games** that **users** can play against **each** other online.
Online **casinos**, sport betting sites, and other forms of gambling are very common.

### Entertainment sharing
Many sites give access to music and video either by allowing users to download copies or **through** streaming (the user can see and **hear** the content but can't keep a **copy**).
Spotify **provides** a streaming service for music, **YouTube** for video.
Some sites **encourage** users to **upload** their own home-made videos for **publication** on the web.
Sites like Flickr allow people to store and publish their own photographs.
In some cases, the sites that offer music or films without the author's consent **are** breaking **copyright** law and are operating illegally;
they use peer-to-peer file sharing **technology** for sending music and films.

## How to build a website
A personal website is your piece of cyberspace.
Anyone can **publish** a website, and people can include what they want.
The basic building blocks are words and pictures, but you can include videos and sound in **your** website.

### What you need...
There are numerous **websites** that offer the possibility of building a website, like Weebly, Wix, and Squarespace.
Most of **these** sites don't require programming skills.
**These** sites **work** with drag-and-drop and point-and-click tools.
You can also follow a template (**a** ready-made layout that you can use).
For hosting a website, you need around 10 Mb.
Your ISP may provide you with that space;
otherwise, you can use **an** external company for hosting a site, like Moonfruit and Google.

### Building the Site
Starting on the home page, you can place a message or a guide with a welcome message **introducing** your website.
You can also include a newsflash on the page to keep the page fresh; you can also divide your site **into** different web pages.
**Pictures** can be **put** on the site **from** a digital file on the computer;
**it** is important to keep **the weight of the pictures small** for preventing **slowdowns in the** loading of the site.
The web is all about links that enable users to jump **to** a different page by clicking on a word;
the jump **redirects** you to another site or a different page from the same **site**.
The page **needs** to be simple for every user; people lose patience **quickly**.

### Protect yourself
Don't include any personal information on the site **because** anyone can read it; **the same applies** to your username and password.

## E-commerce
E-commerce is the conduct of **business through** the internet.
The **buying** can be done from **computer-to-computer**, **via e-mail**, via the web, or **through** smartphone apps.
The payment often involves EFT (**Electronic Funds Transfer**) that is made **in a non-physical** form, such as cash.
Credit **cards** and smart **cards** are **the** key **to** e-commerce; the **important thing** is that the payment is automatic.

### Pros and Cons
E-commerce offers several advantages to **businesses**: the automatic payment **cuts** down costs, and **transactions** are faster and more **convenient**.
**Businesses** can reach wider markets, **potentially worldwide**.
Setting **up e-commerce** is complicated and expensive, and there are also **costs** for publicizing the website.
**E-commerce raises** the problem of fraud.
For the customers, the main attraction is the **ease** and speed of finding products from the **comfort** of their home.
The range of goods is **enormous**, including items **which** might otherwise be very hard to obtain, and the price is often lower;
customers can compare **prices** from **different** suppliers and get news of the product, including **reviews** and reports.

There are certain drawbacks...
**Although** photographs may be included, the customers cannot **physically** examine the product on sale, and many customers are afraid of e-commerce **because** of **fraud**; in addition, customers have to wait for the product to be shipped, and then when it **arrives, there** may be problems about returning the product.

## Use the internet safely

### Install a firewall
A Firewall is a software or **hardware** that **builds** a defensive wall around your computer;
it checks data coming from the network and then **chooses** to block or let **certain types of data pass**.
What the firewall **lets** pass is chosen by configuring **its** settings.

### Keep Software up to date
Most malware **attacks are** made **by criminal gangs** who have found ways of infiltrating known security weak **spots**;
**therefore, it** is important to **install** the **latest** updates offered by the software makers or operating system makers.
Most of the malware **attacks come from** the web browser, so you need to update **that as well**.

### Use protection software
Anti-virus programs scan **e-mails** and programs in the computer and search for **viruses** in them.
If one is found, the program either isolates it or **deletes** it **automatically before** it **creates** damage.
Anti-virus also **scans** existing software to find any viruses present.
New malware is identified every day, so it is important that the anti-virus **has** an automatic update capability.
It is important to never install anti-virus software from **pop-up ads**.

### Take special care with e-mail
Be careful when opening e-mail attachments **because there** can be a virus hidden in the file, and it is important to be careful when clicking on **hyperlinks**; consider **installing** a spam filter to prevent spam **e-mails from** reaching you.
Never **respond** to a spam e-mail; just unsubscribe. Your reply may indicate a confirmation of your e-mail address.
